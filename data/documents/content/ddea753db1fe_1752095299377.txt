HONOLULU (HawaiiNewsNow) - Honolulu police have launched an animal cruelty investigation following suspected feral cat poisoning.
According to police, on Tuesday, July 1, a 48-year-old man reported multiple cases of animal cruelty involving feral cats he cared for.
The man said all the cats had been spayed, neutered, and microchipped.
HPD said the man found the deceased cats on or near his property. The Hawaiian Humane Society has been notified and are assisting in the investigation.
Police said that early indications suggest that the cats could have been poisoned.
HPD is asking anyone with information regarding this matter to call 911 or CrimeStoppers at (808) 955-8300.
